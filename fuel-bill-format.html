What is the Fuel Bill Format Used in India?

Every petrol pump in India prints a receipt in a fairly consistent fuel bill format — a narrow thermal slip with the pump details at the top, transaction fields in the middle, and a thank-you note at the bottom. Whether you fill petrol or diesel, the layout follows the same basic structure, with small differences between oil companies and individual pump dealers.

This page breaks down that format field by field, shows you real fuel bill samples, and explains how the petrol bill format and diesel bill format compare. If you need to create one yourself for personal record keeping, our free generator follows this exact layout.

Petrol Bill Format vs Diesel Bill Format — Key Differences

The petrol bill format and the diesel bill format are almost identical, but a few differences are worth noting:

  • Diesel slips often include a Density (Kg/m³) field that petrol slips usually skip
  • The product field shows "Petrol" or "MS" for petrol, and "Diesel" or "HSD" for diesel
  • Rate per litre is naturally different since diesel is usually priced lower than petrol
  • Premium fuel variants (like Xtra Premium or Speed) appear only on petrol-side receipts

Indian Oil vs HP vs BPCL Fuel Bill Format — What's Different?

All three major oil companies use a similar thermal receipt layout, but each has distinct branding:

  • Indian Oil (IOCL) fuel bill format — IOCL flame logo, "IndianOil" header, dealer code shown prominently
  • HP (HPCL) fuel bill format — HP shield logo, "Hindustan Petroleum" header, HPCL dealer info in footer
  • BPCL fuel bill format — BPCL drop logo, "Bharat Petroleum" header, Petro Card details in footer

The body fields (rate, volume, amount, vehicle number, date, FIP number) are standardised across all three. Generate any of these using our free petrol bill generator or diesel bill generator.

Card Payment vs Cash Fuel Receipt Format

When you pay by card, the fuel bill format adds extra fields such as masked card number, approval code, RRN (Retrieval Reference Number), and "PIN Verified OK." Cash transactions skip these and simply show the payment mode as "Cash." Both formats are supported in our fuel bill generator.

FAQ — Fuel Bill Format India

What is FIP number on a fuel bill? FIP stands for Fuel Inlet Point — it identifies the specific dispenser (machine) at the pump. Combined with Nozzle number, it precisely records which unit dispensed your fuel.

What does MS mean on a petrol bill? MS stands for Motor Spirit — the technical product name for petrol in the Indian oil industry. "Product: MS" on a receipt means petrol.

What does HSD mean on a diesel bill? HSD stands for High Speed Diesel — the standard grade sold at retail pumps for cars, trucks, and generators.

Is the fuel bill format the same across India? Yes, broadly. Since IOCL, HPCL, and BPCL use standardised government-regulated PoS systems, the core receipt fields are the same nationwide. Minor variations exist in dealer name formats and card payment fields.

Fuel Bill Format — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard fuel bill format in India?
+
The standard fuel bill format in India is a thermal PoS receipt that includes the pump name and address, receipt or bill number, FIP and nozzle number, product type, rate per litre, volume in litres, total amount, date and time, and payment mode. Indian Oil, HP, and Bharat Petroleum all follow this same general structure with minor layout differences.
What is the difference between petrol bill format and diesel bill format?
+
The petrol bill format and diesel bill format share the same basic fields — rate, volume, amount, date, vehicle number. Diesel bills sometimes add a density (Kg/m3) field, and the product name may show as Diesel, HSD, or MS for petrol, depending on the oil company's printer template.
What fields appear on a petrol pump receipt format?
+
A petrol pump receipt format typically includes: dealer name and location, receipt or bill number, FIP number, nozzle number, product, rate per litre, volume, total amount, vehicle number, mobile number, date, time, and a thank-you message at the bottom.
Do Indian Oil, HP and Bharat Petroleum use the same bill format?
+
They are similar but not identical. All three use a thermal PoS layout with rate, volume, and amount fields, but the header logo, field labels, and footer text differ slightly between Indian Oil, HP, and Bharat Petroleum.
