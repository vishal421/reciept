Indian Oil Diesel Bill vs HP Diesel Bill vs BPCL Diesel Bill

All three major oil marketing companies in India issue diesel bills in a similar thermal PoS receipt format, but with distinct branding. Indian Oil (IOCL) diesel bills display the IOCL flame logo and dealer code. Hindustan Petroleum (HP) diesel receipts feature the HP shield logo and HPCL dealer information. Bharat Petroleum (BPCL) diesel bills show the Petro Card / BPCL drop logo. Our diesel bill generator faithfully replicates all three layouts — just pick your pump brand and the correct header appears automatically.

What is HSD? — Diesel Bill Format Explained

HSD stands for High Speed Diesel — the technical name used by Indian oil companies (IOCL, HPCL, BPCL) on retail pump receipts. When you see "HSD" on a diesel bill, it refers to the standard diesel sold for cars, trucks, buses, and diesel generators. Some pumps print "Diesel" while others print "HSD"; our generator lets you choose either label to match your actual receipt format.

Diesel bills in India may also include a density reading (Kg/m³) which is a quality parameter — standard HSD density is typically between 820–845 Kg/m³. This field appears on many modern PoS diesel receipts and can be filled in on our generator for accuracy.
